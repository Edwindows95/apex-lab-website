Our approach to work-health intelligence

We believe the most powerful health insights come from connecting what you do at work with how your body actually responds.

The problem
Most people have no idea how their work affects their health. Surveys capture opinions. Check-ups capture snapshots. Neither captures the continuous, objective reality of how your body responds to the demands of every meeting, every deadline, and every late night.

What we believe

1

Objective over subjective

Self-reported stress is unreliable. Garmin wearable data provides continuous, objective measurement of heart rate, stress, sleep, and energy — capturing what you can't feel or report accurately.

2

Context matters

A heart rate of 85 means nothing without context. During a client presentation? Normal. During a quiet afternoon? Potentially concerning. Calendar integration is what makes our data actionable.

3

Privacy is non-negotiable

Individual health data belongs to the individual. Employers never see personal results. This isn't just our policy — it's fundamental to ethical health research and GDPR compliance.

4

Continuous over snapshot

A single blood test or annual check-up captures one moment. Four weeks of continuous data reveals patterns, trends, and the real relationship between your work and your health.

5

Actionable over academic

Beautiful data is worthless if it doesn't lead to change. Every insight we deliver is practical, specific, and something you can act on tomorrow.

Our vision
A world where every professional understands the health cost of their work — and every organization designs work that's sustainable by default.

Built by someone who's been there

Apex Lab was founded by a health and performance professional who saw the gap between what people experience at work and what their body actually goes through. Combining expertise in exercise physiology, wearable technology, and workplace health, Apex Lab bridges that gap with data.
